Yi Yang 杨毅

I am Yi Yang, currently a Ph.D. researcher in Electrical and Computer Engineering at University of Macau, advised by Prof. Feng Wan.

My research focuses on Brain-Computer Interfaces (BCIs), EEG/fNIRS signal analysis, affective computing, and deep learning. I am particularly interested in building adaptive, robust, and privacy-preserving neural interface systems.

Recently, I have been working on class-incremental learning, few-shot learning, domain adaptation, and analytic recursive learning for EEG-based BCIs, including emotion recognition, motor imagery, SSVEP, sleep monitoring, EEG-to-image, and EEG-to-text.

Publications

Unified Online Adaptation Framework for Correlation Analysis-based Spatial Filtering Methods in SSVEP-based BCIs

Ze Wang, Lu Shen, Xinran Mi, Leqian Cheng, Yi Yang, et al.

IEEE Journal of Biomedical and Health Informatics, 2026

This work develops a unified online adaptation framework for correlation-analysis-based SSVEP spatial filtering methods. It aims to transform calibration-based SSVEP decoding algorithms into zero-calibration, continuously adaptive BCI solutions using limited online unlabeled data.

DOIPDFProject

Prototypical Contrastive Learning With Temporal Dynamic Graph Convolutional Network for EEG-Based Emotion Recognition

Yi Yang, Chenxi Sun, Ruoning Lyu, et al., Feng Wan

IEEE Transactions on Affective Computing, 2026

This work proposes PCL-TDGCN for EEG-based emotion recognition, aiming to address non-stationary brain dynamics and cross-subject distribution shifts. It combines temporal dynamic graph modeling with prototypical contrastive learning to capture evolving neural interactions, improve emotion-specific discriminability, and achieve semantic-aware alignment across subjects.

Dual-Branch Attention-based Frequency Domain Network for Cross-subject SSVEP-BCIs

Yi Yang, Ze Wang, Ziyu Jia, Boyu Wang, Shangen Zhang, Chi-Man Wong, Xiaorong Gao, Tzyy-Ping Jung, Feng Wan

IEEE Journal of Biomedical and Health Informatics, 2025

This work proposes DB-AFDNet for cross-subject SSVEP decoding. It independently models real and imaginary spectral components and introduces attention-similarity and orthogonality constraints to learn both shared and branch-specific frequency-domain representations.

Yi Yang, Ze Wang, Yu Song, Ziyu Jia, Boyu Wang, Tzyy-Ping Jung, Feng Wan

IEEE Transactions on Affective Computing, 2025

Ze Wang, Lu Shen, Yi Yang, Yueqi Ma, Chi-Man Wong, Zige Liu, Cuiyun Lin, Chi-Tat Hon, Tao Qian, Feng Wan

Multiple Model Transferability Ensemble Adaptation for Privacy-Preserving BCIs

Yi Yang, Feng Wan

Submitted to IEEE Transactions on Systems, Man, and Cybernetics: Systems (TSMC)

This work studies privacy-preserving cross-subject adaptation for EEG-based BCIs. It combines multiple source models and transferability-aware ensemble learning to improve target-subject performance without requiring direct access to raw source EEG data.

Class Incremental Learning in Brain-Computer Interfaces

Yi Yang, Feng Wan

Submitted to IEEE Transactions on Biomedical Engineering (TBME)

This project formulates class-incremental learning for practical BCI deployment, where new neural intents or categories appear over time. The method aims to mitigate catastrophic forgetting while supporting privacy-preserving and efficient model updates.

PDFCodeProject

Discriminative Few-Shot Analytic Class Incremental Learning for Fast Sleep Stage Classification

Yi Yang, Feng Wan

Submitted to Journal of Biomedical and Health Informatics (JBHI)

This work investigates fast sleep-stage adaptation with only a few labeled samples from novel classes. It focuses on prototype-guided feature expansion and analytic recursive learning to improve stability under few-shot class-incremental settings.

PDFCodeProject

Adaptive Class-wise Multicentric Prototype Learning for Privacy-Preserving BCIs

Yi Yang, Feng Wan

Submitted to Journal of Biomedical and Health Informatics (JBHI)

This work investigates how to improve performance on new participants while protecting individual privacy by utilizing only pre-trained source models and prototype-based pseudo-labels. It focuses on dynamically constructing multiple prototypes for each category to capture non-stationary EEG dynamics, thereby reducing pseudo-label error propagation caused by insufficient feature discrimination.
