About

Research Summary

I am a PhD researcher at the University of Macau, specializing in Brain-Computer Interfaces, affective computing, EEG/fNIRS analysis, and deep learning. My current research explores how intelligent neural interfaces can adapt to new users, new classes, and non-stationary neural patterns while preserving data privacy.

My work connects practical BCI deployment with machine learning topics including transfer learning, domain adaptation, class-incremental learning, few-shot learning, analytic recursive learning, and generative neuro-AI models.

Core Research Goal

Building robust, interpretable, and privacy-preserving BCI systems that require minimal calibration and can continuously adapt to evolving neural data.

Research Interests

Brain-Computer Interfaces

EEG-based BCI systems for affective computing, motor imagery, SSVEP, and neurorehabilitation.

Incremental Learning

Class-incremental and few-shot learning methods for adapting BCI models without catastrophic forgetting.

Privacy-Preserving Learning

Learning algorithms that avoid raw EEG replay and support practical deployment under privacy constraints.

Neural Signal Analysis

EEG/fNIRS signal processing, brain network analysis, MNE, EEGLAB, and statistical evaluation.

Multiple Model Transferability Ensemble Adaptation for Privacy-Preserving BCIs

This work studies privacy-preserving transfer learning for EEG-based BCIs. It leverages multiple source models and dynamic transferability estimation to improve target-subject adaptation without directly accessing raw source-domain EEG data.

BCICIL Continual Learning Submitted

Class Incremental Learning in Brain-Computer Interfaces

This project formulates class-incremental learning for BCI scenarios, where new neural classes or intents are introduced over time. The goal is to reduce catastrophic forgetting while maintaining privacy and practical update efficiency.

  • Supervisor: Prof. Feng Wan
  • Status: Submitted to IEEE Transactions on Biomedical Engineering

Sleep EEG Few-Shot CIL In Preparation

Discriminative Few-Shot Analytic Class Incremental Learning for Fast Sleep Stage Classification

This work investigates fast adaptation for sleep-stage classification under few-shot incremental settings. It aims to stabilize novel-class distributions and improve sleep EEG recognition with limited new-stage calibration samples.

  • Supervisor: Prof. Feng Wan
