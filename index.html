PhD Researcher · Electrical and Computer Engineering · University of Macau

I am a PhD researcher in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Macau, advised by Prof. Feng Wan.

My research focuses on Brain-Computer Interfaces, EEG/fNIRS analysis, affective computing, and deep learning. I am particularly interested in privacy-preserving BCI adaptation, class-incremental learning, few-shot learning, and robust neural signal modeling under non-stationary conditions.

I also explore EEG-based generative neuro-AI topics, including EEG-to-Image and EEG-to-Text, with the broader goal of building adaptive, interpretable, and deployable neural interface systems.

Multiple Model Transferability Ensemble Adaptation for Privacy-Preserving BCIs

Yi Yang, Prof. Feng Wan

Submitted to IEEE Transactions on Systems, Man, and Cybernetics: Systems

This work investigates privacy-preserving target-subject adaptation for EEG-based BCIs. It combines multiple source models and transferability-aware ensemble fusion to exploit reusable source knowledge without directly accessing raw source-domain EEG recordings.

Class Incremental Learning in Brain-Computer Interfaces

Yi Yang, Prof. Feng Wan

Submitted to IEEE Transactions on Biomedical Engineering

This project formulates class-incremental learning for BCI applications where new classes, commands, or neural states are introduced over time. The study emphasizes catastrophic forgetting, privacy constraints, EEG non-stationarity, and efficient model updates without raw replay.

Discriminative Few-Shot Analytic Class Incremental Learning for Fast Sleep Stage Classification

Yi Yang, Prof. Feng Wan

Manuscript in preparation

This work studies rapid sleep-stage classification under few-shot incremental settings. It aims to expand and stabilize novel-class feature distributions with limited calibration samples while using analytic recursive updates for efficient adaptation.
